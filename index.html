  • {{title}}

    I am an associate professor and principal statistician at the Big Data Center for Children's Medical Care, Children's Hospital of Chongqing Medical University. Previously, I was an assistant professor at Nankai University. I received my Ph.D. in Statistics from the University of Toronto in 2012, and I was fortunate to have Prof. Nancy Reid as my advisor for my thesis. After graduation I became a postdoctoral fellow at Dalhousie University (working with Prof. Chris Field). Before going to UofT, I got my bachelor's degree from Nankai University and master's degree from University of Ottawa under the supervision of Prof. David Sankoff.

    My research interests lie primarily in composite likelihood inference and the development of statistical and machine learning methods for analyzing biological and medical data, especially real-world data (RWD) in pediatrics and the data arising from microbiome and environmental health studies, collaborating extensively with pediatricians and biologists.
